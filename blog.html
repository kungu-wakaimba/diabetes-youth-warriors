How I Manage Diabetes as a College Student

By Emily Tran · May 2024

Being diagnosed with Type 1 diabetes at 15 was overwhelming, but it really hit me when I left home for college. Suddenly, I was in charge of my own meals, schedule, and supplies. Here are a few lessons that helped me thrive:

  • Planning ahead for meals and always having healthy snacks on hand.
  • Using phone reminders for insulin and glucose monitoring.
  • Building a support circle by letting friends and my resident advisor know about my diabetes.

Some days are harder than others, but I've learned that advocating for your own needs is a form of strength. If you're a student living with diabetes, remember that you're not alone‚Äîreach out, ask for help, and take care of your wellbeing.

5 Everyday Habits That Improved My Blood Sugar Control

By Rajiv S. · April 2024

Managing Type 2 diabetes took time to figure out, but several simple habits made a big difference in my daily life:

  • Checking my blood sugar at consistent times each day.
  • Eating regular, balanced meals and not skipping breakfast.
  • Walking for 20 minutes after dinner‚Äîit really works!
  • Learning how to read food labels.
  • Practicing mindful breathing to manage stress.

Progress isn't always fast, but small steps add up. Don't underestimate the power of routine, and remember‚Äîit's okay to ask family or friends to help keep you on track.
